Some Of Ozzys Popular Albums

Diary of a Madman

This is Ozzy Osbourne's second solo studio album, released in 1981. It features Randy Rhoads on guitar and is considered a classic in heavy metal. The album includes tracks such as "Diary of a Madman," "Flying High Again," and "Over the Mountain." However, controversy surrounded the album's release due to alterations made to the original master tapes without Osbourne's knowledge.

Ozzmosis

This is Ozzy Osbourne's seventh solo studio album, released in 1995. It features Zakk Wylde on guitar and incorporates alternative and industrial metal elements. The album includes tracks such as "Perry Mason" and "See You on the Other Side," and has gained a cult following among fans. The album artwork, featuring Osbourne holding a baby with three eyes, was created by artist Joe Petagno.

No More Tears

This is Ozzy Osbourne's sixth solo studio album, released in 1991. It was recorded in Los Angeles, produced by Duane Baron, John Purdell, and Ozzy Osbourne himself. The album includes the title track "No More Tears," and was well-received by fans and critics alike. It features contributions from musicians such as Zakk Wylde and Randy Castillo.

Blizzard Of Ozz

This is Ozzy Osbourne's debut solo studio album, released on September 20, 1980. It was recorded in London and features the guitar work of Randy Rhoads. The album includes tracks such as "Crazy Train," "Mr. Crowley," and "Suicide Solution." The album was a critical and commercial success and is considered a classic in heavy metal.

Ordinary Man

This is Ozzy Osbourne's twelfth solo studio album, released on February 21, 2020. It features contributions from musicians such as Elton John, Post Malone, and Slash. The album includes tracks such as "Under the Graveyard," "Straight to Hell," and the title track "Ordinary Man." The album received positive reviews and peaked at number three on the US Billboard 200 chart.

Down To Earth

This is Ozzy Osbourne's eighth solo studio album, released on October 16, 2001. It was recorded in Los Angeles and features the guitar work of Zakk Wylde. The album includes tracks such as "Dreamer," "Gets Me Through," and "Running Out of Time." The album received mixed reviews upon its release but was a commercial success, reaching platinum status in the United States.
